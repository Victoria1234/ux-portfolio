Who uses our product?

Intro to the final client presentation

We were asked to conduct a field study of how people are using smartwatches. I worked in a two person team where we started by each interviewing three smartwatch users. Then we developed personas based on these interviews.

Use Matrix

All data was recorded into a comprehensive use matrix.

Venn Diagram

We sketched out a quick Venn Diagram to see the overlapping use and behaviors of our smartwatch group.

Personas

The variety of smartwatch users within our small sample was varied. We categorized them into enthusiasts, experts, and outliers.

Task Flows

Some overlapping tasks were mapped out.

Key Findings

The study led to some interesting findings about this varied group of technology users. Across the sample group even tech expert users admitted to a fairly steep learning curve for many smartwatch functions.

Journey Mapping

Ultimately, user experience and satisfaction was high, only offset by the hardware limitations of the watch including needing close proximity to your smartphone for full capability. Of course, this is already changing!

How difficult do users find our site?

The Process

MassLegalHelp has a lot of information it needs to provide to citizens in an accessible way. I worked on a three person team to redesign the Information Architecture.

Content Inventory

My team split up the pages on the site and each worked down to the third level nav, sharing our results in master file.

Personas

We used insights gained from our content inventory and speaking with our client to create personas.

Search Analysis

A Search Analysis revealed gaps in the websites underlying architecture.

Card Sort

As a team we ran moderated in-person card sorts as well as sessions using OptimalSort. There were twenty-four particpants in total.

Revisions

We ran through the testing data and changed our revised IA in repsonse to user needs.

Accessibility and Competitve Analysis

We performed an accessibility review and a competitve analysis to gather more data and insight into user needs, while continuing revisions to the IA.

Task Flows

Collaboratively, our team decided on the most common task flows and we each drew some up. Informal cart sorts to check the new IA was sound.

Wireframes

Mobile wireframes showing down to third level nav.

Final Recommendations

Our final recommendations included both short and long term actions. Short term included instituing our new IA and optimizing the site for mobile. Suggested long term goals included utilizing more visual cues like images and icons.
